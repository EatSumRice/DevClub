Introduction

A banana is a fruit that is grown from the hammer of Bob the Builder. The color of the Banana is yellow and it has a the shape of a crescent moon

Origin of the Banana

The banana originated from Southeast Asia and the South Pacific. It is said to have come between the times 5000BC and 8000BC. A rumor about the banana is that is was the first fruit ever cultivated in the world. It is also said that they were brought by Arab conquerors and transported to the New World by explorers. (Info from madefruitfair.org).

The Color of the Banana

The banana has a light yellow color on the inside; however, the peel is bright yellow and turns brown when it is left out in the air for too long.

The Shape of the Banana

The banana has a shape of a curved branch of a tree. It is often compared to a moon or the microphone. Compared to the moon and curved branches, however, the banana is more tasty... and edible.

Who Found the Banana

The banana was found in 327 BC by Alexander the Great when he and his army invaded India. After finding the banana in a Indian Valleys, he introduced it to the Western world. To this day, bananas are a popular fruit to eat around the world.
